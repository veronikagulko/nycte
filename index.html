Classes

We are proud to offer a variety of engineering classes for you!

Lego League Robotics

Get ready to build, code, and compete in our FIRST LEGO League (FLL) Robotics classes! Designed for elementary and middle schoolers, these classes offer an exciting introduction to robotics and programming. Using LEGO kits and guided by expert instructors, students will design and program robots to complete challenges and solve real-world problems.

Mechanical Engineering

Elementary and middle schoolers will explore the principles of mechanics, motion, and energy through fun, hands-on activities. Using interactive kits, students will design, build, and test their own machines, learning how gears, levers, and pulleys make our world work.

Biochemical Engineering

Explore the building blocks of life in our Biochemistry classes! Get hands-on with activities like extracting DNA from fruits, building 3D DNA models, and observing enzymes in action. You'll even dissect organisms to see biochemistry in the real world. Perfect for young scientists eager to dive into the fascinating world of molecules and life!

Aerospace Engineering

Young engineers will explore the science of flight and space exploration through exciting, hands-on projects. With the help of engaging kits, students will build and test their own rockets, gliders, and even mini satellites. Along the way, they'll discover the principles of aerodynamics, propulsion, and the forces that allow aircraft to soar and spacecraft to explore the cosmos.

Benefits

Engineering is not just about the physical aspect of building, it is about learning, teamwork, fun, prototyping, desinging, and connecting with others.

About

Learn about our experience in STEM

  • September 2022

    Our Freshman Year

    In our freshman year, we joined our schools Robotics and Science Olympiad. This was our first true experience in competitive STEM. We soon realized that learning thesev skills at a younger age would have given us more oppurtunities and neccessary background knowledge in our respective fields.

  • March 2024

    Our First STEM Fair

    We joined the efforts of our Robotics teams and Science Olympiad team to host the first STEM Fair at Styvesent High School. With a turn out of over 90%, we hosted 213 attendees and demonstrated our labs and mechanisms.

  • July 2024

    Our FLL Teams

    We began volunteering at a local community center to teach elementary and middle school children the foundations of FFL Robotics. During these weekly claases we built over 20 robots such as, a locking safe and fitness tracker.

  • August 2024

    NYCT&E Launch

    We discovered a large demand for STEM classes in our local area. While most programs focus on purely academic tutoring, we want the focus towards classes that will expand on childrens hobbies and benefit them in the future.

Learning

FLL Chinatown

Learning block coding provides young kids with an accessible introduction to the world of programming. By using a visual interface where they drag and drop blocks to create sequences, children can grasp foundational coding concepts like loops, conditionals, and variables without the complexity of typing syntax. This approach simplifies the learning process, allowing kids to focus on the logic behind coding rather than getting bogged down by technical details.

  • Tools: Code.org
  • Age: Elementary School

Teamwork

FLL Bensonhurst

Teaching teamwork in FIRST LEGO League (FLL) teams helps students develop crucial collaboration skills. As they design, build, and program robots together, they learn effective communication, appreciate diverse ideas, and solve problems creatively. This experience fosters responsibility and prepares them for future success in group projects and professional environments.

  • Tools: Spike Prime Kit
  • Age: Elementary - Middle School

Prototyping

Science Olympiad

Prototyping is a crucial skill in STEM education as it allows students to bring abstract ideas to life, testing and refining them in a tangible way. Through prototyping, students learn to iterate on their designs, improving problem-solving abilities and fostering creativity. It also helps them understand the practical challenges and constraints of real-world engineering and design. This hands-on approach not only reinforces theoretical knowledge but also builds critical thinking and adaptability, preparing students for future innovation and technological development

  • Tools: Scioly Tech Builds
  • Age: Those are our builds! So all!

Designing

FLL Bensonhurst

The FIRST LEGO League (FLL) uses LEGO bricks as a fun, hands-on way for students to design and build innovative solutions to real-world problems. Through this process, participants learn that engineering isn't just about calculations—it's about creativity and thinking outside the box. By combining engineering principles with imaginative design, FLL shows that solving challenges can be as playful as it is purposeful.

  • Tools: Spike Prime Kit
  • Age: Elementary School

Connecting

FLL Manhattan

Student mentors in STEM classes create meaningful connections with younger students, serving as relatable role models and guides. These mentors bring STEM to life outside the classroom by sharing real-world experiences and hands-on learning opportunities. This peer-to-peer interaction not only deepens understanding but also fosters a supportive community where passion for STEM can flourish.

  • Tools: Spike Prime Kit
  • Age: Elementary School
